5.1 Description

Based on the leaked credentials and the identified compromised employees and clients records, we recommend the following mitigation plan, separated for Employees and Clients.

5.2 Employees

The identified employees credentials are used to login to the company applications and other services, therefore, we recommend to:

  • Password Reset: Reset the compromised employees passwords for both Active Directory and for local applications;
  • Disable Chrome password sync for corporate laptops:When employees are logged-in to Google Chrome from their corporate laptop, their passwords are automatically synced to their personal Gmail accounts. When employees personal laptop/phone gets compromised, all synced passwords, including corporate credentials are stolen. Therefore, we highly recommend to disable Google chrome login and password sync. Following steps explains how to disable the Google Chrome login and password sync:
    • Open Google Chrome on your laptop;
    • Click on the three-dot menu icon located at the top-right corner of the browser window;
    • From the dropdown menu, select "Settings";
    • In the Settings tab, scroll down and click on "Sync and Google services";
    • Under the "Sync" section, click on "Sync" again;
    • In the "Sync settings" window, toggle off the switch next to "Passwords" to disable password sync;
    • Additionally, you can toggle off the switch next to "Autofill" to disable other autofill data sync if desired;
      Note: the above steps must be applied company wide.
  • Initiate security awareness program: Start a security awareness program to teach employees how to stay secure online and avoid downloading malicious or untrusted software. At Dark Entry, we have a security awareness platform that contains video materials and practical assignments for the employees to make sure they are on track and to sharpen their security awareness skills. We are happy to help, contact us ( sales@darkentry.net ) for more details of our security awareness and training platform.

5.3 Clients

We understand that the cybersecurity team at xrasco.com wouldn’t have access to mitigate the data breach at the client’s side. However, communicating the identified issues is the key to demonstrate that xrasco.com is ahead of the game when it comes to clients security.

Therefore we recommend performing the following steps:

  • Enable 2FA: Make sure that all internet-facing applications and services requires 2-step verification;
  • Password reset:As the clients credentials are already leaked, we recommend to reset the client password and inform the client accordingly to reset their passwords.
  • Communication and co-ordination: To make sure the mitigation plan is aligned and the clients are informed, we recommend to construct a list of all the compromised emails and communicate with the clients to explain and alert them of their credentials being exposed in the Dark Web. The email communication should include the following:
    • Explain the situation:
    • i.e. Our Threat Intelligence team identified credentials exposure in the Dark web related to your company/employees..)
    • Actions: Inform the clients of the actions taken by xrasco.com to eliminate the risk (i.e. reset the user password, enforced 2FA and the performed incident handling process)
    • Expectations: Explain your expectations to the clients, such as, the client has to pick a complex password, re-install the operating system to erase the malware, make sure their operating system and software are always up-to-date and a periodical malware scan is performed and ask them to avoid downloading software from untrusted sources.
